Expanded Technical Framework for DORA Compliance

Published by Security Expert • 5 min read

Building upon the foundational risk management requirements, the Digital Operational Resilience Act (DORA) mandates specific technical obligations regarding third-party oversight, advanced testing, and collaborative intelligence.

4. ICT Third-Party Risk Management

Financial entities must maintain rigorous oversight of ICT service providers to ensure the security of the entire supply chain.

  • Contractual Transparency: Contracts must include comprehensive service-level descriptions (SLDs) and explicit identification of data processing locations.
  • Monitoring Accessibility: Entities must ensure that contracts grant them full monitoring and audit rights, enabling constant oversight of the provider’s operational status.
  • Union Oversight: Critical third-party providers are subject to a unified EU-level oversight framework to harmonize supervisory approaches across member states.

5. Advanced Resilience Testing

Testing requirements are proportionate to an entity’s size, business profile, and risk exposure.

  • TLTP (Threat-Led Penetration Testing): Entities with higher risk profiles must conduct "Red/Purple Teaming" assessments. These tests simulate real-world cyberattacks to validate the effectiveness of defensive controls.
  • Remediation Lifecycle: Any deficiencies or gaps identified during testing must be systematically documented and mitigated through counteractive measures.

6. Information Sharing and Collaboration

DORA promotes the exchange of cyber threat intelligence among financial entities to bolster collective defense.

  • Trusted Communities: Entities are encouraged to share information regarding threat vectors, detection techniques, and mitigation strategies.
  • Confidentiality: Intelligence sharing must occur via arrangements that protect the sensitive nature of proprietary and customer-related data.

Summary

DORA extends beyond internal governance by mandating strict third-party oversight, high-fidelity threat-led penetration testing, and industry-wide intelligence sharing. As of January 17, 2025, financial institutions must ensure their risk management frameworks are fully aligned with these technical requirements to avoid significant regulatory penalties.
